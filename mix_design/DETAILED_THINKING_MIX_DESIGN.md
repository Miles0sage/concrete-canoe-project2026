# DETAILED THINKING: HOW WE GOT MIX 3
## NAU Concrete Canoe Mix Design Evolution & Decision Process

---

## PART 1: THE THINKING PROCESS (Step-by-Step)

### 1.1 Initial Problem Statement

**Goal:** Design lightweight concrete for floating canoe

**Constraints:**
- Density: 48-65 PCF (must float)
- Strength: >= 1,500 psi (flexural)
- Timeline: 28 days to full strength
- Cost: Minimize (competition budget)
- Workability: Good flow for thin walls
- Durability: Water-resistant (boat in water)

### 1.2 Decision Chain (How We Narrowed Choices)

**Step 1: CEMENTITIOUS MATERIAL SELECTION**
- Question: Portland only? Or mixed binders?
- Analysis: 100% Portland = fast but expensive
- Analysis: Slag + Fly Ash = slower early, stronger long-term, cheaper
- Decision: **MIXED BINDERS** (Portland + Slag + Fly Ash)
- Rationale: Cost savings + slag durability benefit

**Step 2: PORTLAND CEMENT PERCENTAGE**
- Question: How much Portland needed?
- ASCE Rule: <= 40% of total cementitious
- Analysis: Minimum viable = ~30% (enough for early strength)
- Analysis: Too low (<20%) = too slow
- Decision: **30.8% Portland** (97.21 lbs / 315.92 total)
- Rationale: Just above minimum, balances cost + early strength

**Step 3: SLAG SELECTION**
- Question: Why slag over other pozzolans?
- Data: NAU 2019 used slag successfully (1,950 psi @ 28d)
- Data: Slag provides sustained strength (28d+ benefit)
- Cost: Slag ~$0.25/lb vs Fly Ash ~$0.15/lb (trade-off OK)
- Performance: Slag gives +100-200 psi vs fly ash alone
- Decision: **47.2% SLAG** (149.21 lbs)
- Rationale: Proven in lightweight concrete, long-term strength

**Step 4: FLY ASH INCLUSION**
- Question: How much fly ash?
- Workability Benefit: Fly ash improves flowability
- Cost Benefit: Cheapest binder (~$0.15/lb)
- Decision: **18.9% FLY ASH** (59.78 lbs)
- Rationale: Fills slot between Portland + Slag, aids workability

**Step 5: HYDRATED LIME ADDITION**
- ASCE Rule: <= 5% of total
- Function: Improves plasticity, reduces bleeding
- Decision: **3.1% LIME** (9.72 lbs)
- Rationale: Improves workability, within ASCE limit

**Step 6: LIGHTWEIGHT AGGREGATE SELECTION**

| Aggregate | SG | Size | Function | Cost |
|-----------|-----|------|----------|------|
| Poraver (1-2mm) | 0.296 | Medium | Primary structural | ~$2.50/lb |
| Perlite (0.1-1mm) | 0.125 | Fine | Void filler, density optimizer | ~$1.50/lb |
| K1 Glass Bubbles | 0.125 | Fines | Strength boost, sand replacement | ~$3.00/lb |

Decision: **MIX ALL THREE** -- each plays specific role

**Step 7: WATER-CEMENT RATIO**
- Decision: **w/cm = 0.36 WITH HRWR**
- 0.36 is very dry (tight mix) but maximizes durability + strength
- HRWR (MasterGlenium 7500) maintains workability

**Step 8: ADMIXTURES**
- MasterGlenium 7500 (HRWR): 6 fl oz/cwt -- allows low w/c without losing workability
- MasterAir AE 200: 1.5 fl oz/cwt -- air entrainment for durability + lighter weight

**Step 9: FIBER REINFORCEMENT**
- Master Fiber 150 (PVA): 4.34 lbs -- crack bridging, post-crack strength

**Step 10: CURING STRATEGY**
- Standard 28-day curing (with optional CO2 acceleration)
- Slag provides sustained strength development

## PART 3: TECHNICAL JUSTIFICATION FOR EACH COMPONENT

### Portland Cement (97.21 lbs, 30.8%)

| Portland % | 7-Day | 28-Day | Assessment |
|-----------|-------|--------|------------|
| 25% | ~200 psi | ~1,400 psi | Too slow |
| **30%** | **~273 psi** | **~1,774 psi** | **Optimal** |
| 40% | ~350 psi | ~2,100 psi | Too fast, brittle |

### Slag Cement (149.21 lbs, 47.2%)

- Costs less ($0.25/lb vs Portland $0.35/lb)
- Gains strength continuously 7-28 days
- NAU 2019 used 40-50% successfully (1,950 psi)
- Provides better durability than Portland-only

### Lightweight Aggregates (258.34 lbs total)

- **Poraver** = structural backbone
- **Perlite** = density optimizer (removes water easily)
- **K1** = strength boost (where density matters)
- **Total** = optimized for both density AND strength

### Water-Cement Ratio (0.36)

- Very dry but with HRWR = best of both worlds
- High strength + Good workability
- Low permeability (critical for boat in water)

## CONCLUSION

Starting Point: Need Lightweight (55-65 PCF) + Strong (1,500+ psi) + Durable

1. Material Selection -> Mixed binders (Portland + Slag + Fly Ash)
2. Optimization -> Minimize Portland (cost), maximize Slag (durability)
3. Aggregate Design -> Combination of three aggregates for balance
4. Durability -> Low w/c ratio (0.36) for waterproofing
5. Workability -> HRWR to maintain flow despite low w/c

**Result: MIX 3**
- 58.6 PCF (optimal density)
- 1,774 psi predicted (safe margin)
- Low permeability (durable)
- Good workability (constructible)
- BEST CHOICE FOR COMPETITION
